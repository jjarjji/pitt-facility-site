HVAC Optimization: Cut Energy Costs 25% Without Replacing Equipment

March 1, 2026 · 10 min read

Most commercial buildings run their HVAC systems at 40-60% inefficiency. The equipment itself is fine — the problem is configuration, sequencing, and control settings that haven't been optimized since the building was commissioned. This guide covers eight optimizations that consistently deliver 20-30% energy savings in commercial buildings without requiring any capital equipment replacement.

The Data: Where Energy Goes in a Commercial Building

In a typical commercial building in Pennsylvania: HVAC accounts for 35-45% of total energy use; lighting 15-20%; water heating 8-12%; plug loads 15-25%; and other systems 5-10%. The HVAC number is almost always higher than it needs to be because of common configuration and control problems that are cheap to fix.

Optimization 1: Supply Air Temperature Reset (Savings: 8-12%)

Most commercial buildings cool to a fixed supply air temperature (typically 55°F) year-round. But buildings rarely need 55°F air — most need 58-62°F in moderate weather. Supply air temperature reset (SATR) automatically raises the cooling setpoint as outdoor conditions allow, reducing compressor runtime significantly. Most building automation systems support SATR natively; the configuration takes a technician 2-4 hours and costs nothing beyond labor.

Optimization 2: Optimized Start/Stop Scheduling (Savings: 5-10%)

Buildings frequently start conditioning space 1-2 hours before occupancy and continue for 1-2 hours after everyone leaves. Optimized start/stop uses outdoor temperature and building thermal mass modeling to determine the minimum run time needed to achieve comfort by occupancy start. Average savings: 5-10% of HVAC energy. Payback: 6-18 months depending on current schedule inefficiency.

Optimization 3: Outdoor Air Damper Reset (Savings: 3-6%)

Constant outdoor air dampers — common in older buildings — bring in the same amount of outdoor air at 10°F as at 75°F. This wastes enormous amounts of energy heating and cooling outdoor air that's far more than needed for ventilation. Economizer-based outdoor air reset uses outdoor air temperature to modulate outdoor air intake, bringing in more outdoor air when it's beneficial (cool, dry days) and minimizing it when it's costly (hot, humid days).

Optimization 4: Fan Curve Optimization for VAV Systems (Savings: 4-8%)

Variable air volume (VAV) systems with variable speed drives (VFDs) frequently run fans at higher speeds than needed because the static pressure setpoint was never optimized. Reducing static pressure setpoint to the minimum needed to maintain adequate airflow at the most demanding VAV box typically reduces fan energy by 20-40%. The optimization involves walking the building with a technician measuring airflow at VAV boxes and adjusting the static pressure setpoint accordingly.
